Hey, I'm Ian.
It's nice to meet you.

I design strategies, implement projects, and build solutions for social impact entrepreneurs, NGOs, and grassroots organizations. I specialize in social innovation design, data management and analysis, machine learning, internal capacity-building, and most recently, have dove head into cybersecurity.


Over the last 10 years, I've worked on a number of projects and teams that support communities at a local level and a global scale. My approach and philosophy is shaped and defined by the 3 years I spent at the Self-Employed Women's Association, learning under the tutelege of community, labor, and gender advocates and leaders Ela Bhatt, Renana Jhabvala, and Sanchita Mitra, and the experiences I had at scaling social innovation technology solutions at MIT Solve. Since then, I've been a part of a variety of projects and teams, such as improving the cybersecurity infrastructure a human rights archive in Yemen and Syria; designing and launching a community advocacy solution in Peru; creating Connecticut's first digital tool to understand policy issues for people facing homelessneess; and many others.


I am a graduate of Berkeley's School of Information Masters of Information Management Systems program. I received a graduate certificate in Information and Communication Technology for Development (ICTD) and I am an inaugral member of the Digital Transformation of Development (DToD) Fellowship, an NSF Research Traineeship (NRT) Program. I am also proud graduate from The Ohio State University with degrees in International Development, Economics, and French, and continue to support the Buckeye Leadership Fellows.

Outside of work, I love baseball and college football. I collect baseball cards and memorabilia like they're going out of style and my fall Saturdays are spent waking up at 6am to watch my beloved Buckeyes. I love to tinker and experiment with new recipes and hangin out with my 21 year old cat, PJ.
